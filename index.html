About Me

Hello! I'm Abhay Kumar Prusty, a final-year Integrated BS-MS student at IISER Kolkata majoring in Physical Sciences. My research interests lie in observational and theoretical astrophysics—especially cosmic evolution, spiral arm structure, exoplanetary science, and the Epoch of Reionization.

I’ve interned at institutions like TIFR, worked with data from Gaia, LAMOST, and LOFAR, and contributed to citizen science platforms like Zooniverse. I'm also passionate about space sustainability, and I actively engage in science communication through @thespacebouy.

Research

My research spans across galactic structure, stellar populations, and cosmology. I've worked on:

  • Spiral Arm Mapping: Developing SpiralMap, a Python package to model the Milky Way's spiral arms using masers, H II regions, and Gaia OB stars.
  • 21-cm Cosmology: Studying the Epoch of Reionization and forecasting signals using semi-numeric simulations and Lyman-alpha emitters.
  • Astrometric Surveys: Using Gaia DR3 and chemical abundances to reconstruct the Milky Way’s merger history.

I also explore radio astronomy, citizen science classifications, exoplanet interiors using Love numbers, and gravitational wave cosmology.
